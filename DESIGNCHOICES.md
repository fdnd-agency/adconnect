# DESIGN CHOICES 

In this document, we explain the design choices our team made throughout the project. These decisions reflect our shared vision for usability, accessibility, and visual consistency. Each choice was made with the end user in mind, ensuring that the design not only looks cohesive but also supports a smooth and intuitive experience.

We discuss how our visual style, color palette, typography, layout, and interaction patterns contribute to the overall identity of the product. By documenting these design choices, we aim to make our process transparent and provide a clear rationale behind every decision made during the design and development phases.
___

## COLORPALLETE 

We chose blue and orange as our primary colors because they create a strong visual contrast against white, improving readability and accessibility. The blue represents trust, reliability, and professionalism qualities that are essential for an educational platform. According to [Color Psychology](https://www.colorpsychology.org/blue/)
 research, blue is often associated with calmness, intelligence, and confidence, which supports the school’s goal of building credibility and a sense of security for its audience.

In contrast, orange adds warmth and energy. It symbolizes creativity, enthusiasm, and approachability, which helps make the website feel more youthful and engaging for a younger audience. As studies on [color emotion](https://www.colorpsychology.org/orange/)
suggest, orange can evoke friendliness and motivation balancing the professionalism of blue with a modern, dynamic edge.

By combining these two colors, we created a palette that feels both trustworthy and vibrant, appealing to a broad audience that includes students, educators, and potential partners. This combination also aligns with the school’s identity: professional and credible, but still open, modern, and inspiring.

# #LOGO 

We decided to update the existing logo because the previous design felt outdated and did not align with the new visual direction of the website. The original name, “Overlegplatform Associate Degree,” was long and difficult to read at smaller sizes, which affected usability and brand recognition.

To modernize the brand, we shortened the name to “AdConnect.” Shorter names are easier to remember, more useable for digital use, and create a stronger visual impact.

We also redesigned the logo to match our new color palette (blue and orange), reinforcing consistency across the brand. The typography and positioning were adjusted to center the name beneath the icon, creating a more balanced and professional composition.

This new logo better represents the platform’s identity while remaining visually aigned with the updated design of the website.

## TYPOGRAPHY

We chose Clash Display Variable for headings and Archivo for body text.
Clash Display gives our titles a modern and distinctive look, helping them stand out and reflect the platform’s creative side.
Archivo is clean and highly readable, making it ideal for longer texts and ensuring accessibility across devices.
Together, they create a professional yet approachable visual style that fits the tone of the website.

## MASCOTE 

Because we were asked to make the platform feel more youthful, we designed a mascot — a bird with glasses. Birds are often associated with education and wisdom, like owls in children’s books, which symbolize learning and intelligence.
Our bird carries a book about Associate Degrees, connecting directly to the platform’s purpose while adding a friendly and playful touch. This mascot helps make the website more approachable and engaging for students and will be featured on the student-related pages to strengthen the youthful, accessible identity of the brand.

## NAVIGATION

We decided to create two navigation systems to make the website clearer for different user groups.
The top navigation allows users to select which group they belong to, while also showing pages that are relevant to all target groups.
Below that, we added a secondary navigation specific to each group, helping users find content that matches their needs more easily.
This structure improves usability and orientation, ensuring that every visitor whether student, teacher, or partner can quickly find the right information.
